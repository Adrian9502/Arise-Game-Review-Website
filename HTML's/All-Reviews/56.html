Project : Blood Strike Review

All you need to know.

BY CASPER TINZS

Project: BloodStrike was released early this year. This game is a battle royale (BR) type of game that lets you play in a huge map structure with different graphics and new tactical weapons. It is interesting to say Project: BloodStrike is the same as PUBG and CODM branding games which provide their fans BR and MP as well. This year was the emergence of new, top, and optimised immersive and FPS games. Same story goes for the upcoming game Call of Duty®: Warzone™ Mobile from Activision Publishing, Inc.. Every one of these games regardless of their releases has worked on their own graphic quality, realistic immersive experience, and tactical movements along with the improvements in maps, weapons, and bugs. Every pre-register game has to work on its servers and the bugs in the beta tests so that players will gain more confidence for the global launch to play because they will already know that the developers will work on the game so well. Project: BloodStrike is no exception. NetEase Games published the first beta test for Project: BloodStrike on Google Play Store in February and allowed limited players to join the beta test for their valuable feedback. Project: BloodStrike is a first-person action shooter game that only focuses on BR with higher graphical quality and realistic gameplay. One of the advantages this game brought about is the accessibility of high-end and low-end devices that Project: BloodStrike can turn the tide of downloading and installing compared to the other high graphic games that will be published soon. This article will show you more details on the game mechanics, the story, characters, and gameplay, and how to pre-register to have a chance of playing Project: BloodStrike from others.

This game, like any other fast-paced BR games, has the same category and mechanism in regard to killing common enemies. Little we know is the fact that Project: BloodStrike is inspired by popular BR games. However, this FPS game has more advantages compared to other games mentioned earlier.

Project: BloodStrike like any other top BR games has its own unique map that you drop down from your flight and start your fight with a pistol. One thing you should know is the mechanism of reviving your teammates and buying stations. By collecting cash from the map, you can not only buy your teammates back the whole game as unlimited respawns but also get upgraded weapons and perks to upgrade your operator.

During the beta test, Project: BloodStrike has released 12 weapons that categorised as pistols, ARs, SMGs, shotguns, and sniper rifles namely Glock, Desert Eagle, M4A1, Ak 47, Vector, KAG-6, URB, INP9, Origin-12, MP155, M700, and Kala. These weapons can get cool and fresh skins as well. Also, you can design your own weapon built in the loadout drops.

Project: BloodStrike offered 8 special and powerful characters that have distinctive abilities in the battlefield unlike other games that design characters for the sake of its beauty. This section will be discussed in detail in the next part.

There is one important part of the game that separates it from other FPS games and it is the graphics. Project: BloodStrike tried to create the best graphics design that not only helps low-end devices to play so smoothly but also share the ultimate new generation graphics to perform better in high-end devices with 120 FPS.

Project Bloodstrike Story and Operators Skills

In fact, Project: BloodStrike does not offer a special story unique to its gameplay. Instead, it is inspired by other franchises and immersive games that incorporate elements from various maps and game modes which is BR. In terms of narratives, Project: BloodStrike centres on providing their fans with amazing and stunning competitive experiences. While the game follows a single line, the core gameplay does not have a story campaign. However, the players have to wait for the global launch to see if any other modes or story will be added to the game or not.

Characters in the game or operators have special abilities when closing to enemies. Project: BloodStrike attached one special skill and ability to each operator so that they can deploy obstacles to defend, place turrets to assist your teammates while covering behind a wall. There are 8 operators with their unique characteristics which are as follows:

Ethan (Protection Barrier): By placing a shield in front of himself, he can use that cover to his advantage or help his

teammates to revive when they don’t have a self-revive kit.

Jet (Missile Bombardment): A certain circle will be covered in an atomic bomb that does not let the enemy run away from it.

ala (Toxic Grenade): He throws a toxic grenade through the enemy so that he can take them down so quickly.

Val (Satellite Scan): This operator has the ability of finding the enemies within 100 metres near so that you can ambush and knock down targets easily.

Hank (Heavy Fire Turret): Other than placing turrets to assist the teammates, Hank can collect skill mods from the dead enemy loot boxes to upgrade his turret for more fire power.

Spike (Assassination State): He performs the act of assassinating the enemies when you are in a close range with them.

Kraken (Abyss): Project: BloodStrike was inspired by Valorant for this operator in that this character throws a smoke-like grenade to blind his enemies when they get close to him and trap them inside the circle so that he can shoot easily without them noticing.

E.M.T (Healing Beacon): he works as a medic and puts a medic box around his teammates to heal them up so well.

The close beta test is only available for residents of 15 countries which are the United States, Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Chile, Peru, Colombia, Saudi Arabia, United Arab Emirates, Egypt, Turkey, Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and The Philippines.. After the first release of Project: BloodStrike in these areas, its Google Play Store was open for only limited players who could join the game for rating and reviewing the gameplay. Moreover, there is one more option to get the beta test servers is to join Project: BloodStrike official Discord server for further information about the second beta test or global launch. In this case, if you want to play Project: BloodStrike before the global launch, you should head to the Google Play Store on GameLoop or your smartphone to pre-register.
