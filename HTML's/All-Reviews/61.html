Farlight 84 Review

Kill em all.

BY CASPER TINZS

The mobile battle royale game Farlight 84 was supposed to come out in 2020, but the date has been pushed back many times since then. It came out in 2021 and went into beta for an undetermined amount of time. Two years later, we almost thought it would come out in 2084, which is the age of its virtual world and where it gets its name. But in the end, the teams’ patience has paid off! On April 24, 2023, version 1.0 of Farlight 84 came out all over the world, which made its people very happy.

Farlight 84 has been in beta for 2 years, which has helped it build a strong base before it comes out on mobile. It’s time to join its post-apocalyptic world, which is similar to PUBG mobile’s New State, and make a killing in multiplayer! On the battlefield, you don’t play as a blank avatar, but as one of the game’s 14 iconic figures with your own skin. Race against your opponents while wearing your best jetpack and dodging their shots from the air. Fight with weapons and cars like the crab and the war spider that are one of a kind.

During the game, you find upgrades and other things, just like in Fortnite and most other BR games for mobile. No matter what game mode you play, your goal is to be the last shot still alive. In fact, when Farlight 84 came out, it had more game modes than just the fight royale. Some of these are deathmatchs and group rallies.

Is Farlight 84 Safe for Kids?

For those who like fast-paced hero battle royale games, Farlight 84 is the place to be. There don’t seem to be any explicit content issues with the game itself, but the safety for kids may rely on their own tastes and their parents’ comfort level with battle royale games. It’s important to remember that online multiplayer games can let players connect with other people.

To keep kids safe while they play, it’s best to watch their gameplay, communication features, and in-game interactions. With any online game, it’s important for parents to keep an eye on their kids and know how the community features work so that they can play safely.

Farlight 84 Characters

MAGGIE

Many people shouldn’t be surprised that Maggie is the best character in Farlight 84. This character is a favorite of a lot of players, and the reason is that she can move around so well. Jet-Boost, her first ability, lets her move faster after using a Jetpack. Her final ability, which lets her shorten the time it takes for a Jetpack to recharge, is one of the best in the game.

Maggie’s second ability, “Blast Delivery,” is the only one of her abilities that does damage. However, her other abilities give her a lot of mobility, which is a very powerful tool that many skilled players can use to their advantage. This is why she is thought to be the best character in Farlight 84 right now.

MR.K

There are a lot of jokes about M.K.R. being an army of one robot because he is one of the best characters in Farlight 84. The reasons for that are kind of like why Maggie is getting her jetpack fixed so she can move around more.

This is made possible by his first ability, Down’n Dut, which restarts the Jetpack cooldown after a kill or an assist. M.K.R. has a lot of mobility, but he also does damage with Spray n’ Pray, which lets him skip the ammo limit and just keep firing, and his powerful ultimate, Fatal Volley, which does the same thing but in a wider area.

CEANA

It’s cool that Ceana can deal damage and move quickly, but many groups would love to have her on their team because of her ability Emergency Aid, which saves people faster. Restoration Field is also very useful for healing and repairing shields, and her ultimate, Medi-Ex, which sends out drones that can heal or save people, is useful when your friends just want to rush in.

MOMOI

While Ceana is definitely the superior option when it comes to keeping the team up, Momoi is not so bad at it either thanks to her Quick Aid passive that allows her and her allies to use Med Kits and Restore Shields faster. To help the use of consumables, she can also deploy a Smoke Bomb as a distraction that obscures vision in the thrown area, and her ultimate ability Hoarder Drop will help her team get kitted out faster than other teams.

CAPTAIN

The Captain’s passive ability, Explosion-Proof, isn’t very exciting, but it can save him when he’s taking a lot of AoE damage because it lowers it. If you like to play it safe, his Barrier Deployment lets you heal up quickly under the barrier and bring enemies in for a close fight. His ultimate ability, Aqua Blast, gives you a water gun that slows foes and does good damage. He might not be the best character in Farlight 84, but also not the worst.

SUNIL

If you like to spray and move around while you do it, you might like his passive Adrenaline, which makes you move when you shoot. It’s fine, even though it’s not as good as Maggie’s Jetpack restart from Farlight 84. He does have Springboard, which is like a rocket because anyone who steps on it can fly away. The last ability, “Enhanced Hologram,” is great for when you want to go all-in or just run away, since it gets rid of bad effects and makes the team move faster and deal more damage.

BEAU

As we get closer to the end of the Farlight 84 tier list, you’ll start to see figures who can’t move around. Beau’s passive skill, Assault Rifle Mod Master, makes it so that Assault Rifles can hold more bullets in their magazines. Even though that could be helpful sometimes, skilled players won’t need those extra shots to destroy their enemies. His other two abilities, Frenzy Blast and Cluster Granade, can be dodged or used against him by skilled players. Frenzy Blast does some damage like a grenade would, and Cluster Granade is just more explosions.

SYFER

Putting Syfer on the tier list wasn’t easy because her passive ability, SMG Pro, isn’t the best because it just makes it faster to reload an SMG. Area Recon, her other skill, is also not very good because it only shows enemies who are already close. But we put Syfer in eighth place because of her ultimate, EMP Shock, which turns off devices, breaks shields, and, most importantly, makes high-tier heroes unable to use their Jetpacks.

DUCKYSYDE

If this Farlight 84 list were based on how it looks, Duckysyde would be at the top without any trouble. But he is only at number nine when it comes to his skills. His natural ability, “Double Restore,” makes a Shield Recharger work twice as well. This can be very useful, but sometimes you die before you can use it. Momoi’s ability, Smoke Bomb, is the same, and there’s nothing interesting about it. His ultimate, Travel Barrier, is just a defensive ability that lets him use his passive ability better.

WATCHMAN

Defense characters have never been the best choice in battle royale games because they can’t push. Because of this, Watchman is in the tenth spot. When he takes damage, his passive Auto-Guard makes an energy shield that players can just wait out. Bunkers Away puts up a wall that players can go around, and his ultimate, the Freeze Defense Turret, is also something that players can just walk away from.

YONG

With Track ‘n Trace, you can sometimes find it helpful to see the enemy’s footprints, but most of the time, you can already hear the enemy when they are close, so it is a useless passive. Yong’s second ability is to place a Springboard that launches the user into the air. This is cool, but not as good as a Jetpack restart, and his ultimate, Divulge, which gives information about a certain area, is not as good as other ultimates.

LUCINDA

Shotguns aren’t usually the best weapons in shooters, which is why Lucinda’s passive ability, “Shotgun Assault,” is thought to be one of the worst. All it does is make shotguns have bigger magazines and reload faster. Her skills The Portable Incendiary Bomb is a bomb that does damage over time, which is bad if players can move away. The same goes for her final move, Absolute Gravity, which doesn’t pull people in as well as it should.

PHANTOM

Phantom has a passive skill called “Fatal Shot,” which sounds great because headshots from Sniper Rifles do more damage. But it’s usually hard to do this, or it’s just too much to begin with. Her second ability, Portable Scout, isn’t very good because it shows where she is better than where the enemy is. Her ultimate, Optic Camo, is great for ambushes but is useless at long range.

EMBER

What makes the Farlight 84 best heroes are their mobility, power, and maybe their ability to stay alive. Ember, on the other hand, has none of those things because all of his powers are related to seeing. His passive Eye of Truth makes him resistant to Smoke Bombs, his second ability is a Smoke Bomb, and his ultimate, Vision Erosion, is just a useless ability that tells other teams where your team is, since those who are near you already know where you are.

Final Thoughts

The Farlight 84 review says that the game could become the next PUBG. The review says that the game has great graphics and an interesting world, but it also says that there are some problems with getting goods. This means that the game has some good things about it, but it might need some work in some areas to reach its full potential.
