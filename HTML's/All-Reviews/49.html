League of Legends : Wild Rift Review

Buying mobility boots.

BY CASPER TINZS

A huge part of my teenage years were spend behind my PC playing Call of Duty and League of Legends, and after CoD Mobile convinced me that a shooter on mobile can work and be enjoyable, League of Legends Wild Rift shows me that even the MOBA I grew to have a love/hate relationship over the years plays awesome on mobile. This weekend I got the chance to test the game early in Europe, while being available already in other parts of the world, and enjoyed it so much that I honestly couldn't stop playing. I wanted to unlock all my favorite characters and get some skins for them through the free events, and before I knew it, I was hooked again. Playing League of Legends on a phone might seem like a bad idea, but it works so, so much better than expected.

League of Legends Wild Rift is for the most part the pc version on the game, put on mobile, with a couple optimalisation options to streamline the experience. You won't use your mouse and keyboard, instead, you control your character with a thumbstick like feature on the left side of the screen, while your spells, items, summoner spells and other options are on the right. Controlling your character and throwing out spells needs some practice, since you cannot throw them out as fast as on PC, at least, without a lot of practice. After a couple games I noticed I was getting more comfortable with this, but throwing out an e first before I flash as Ahri to engage, seemed a bit too hard for me still. If you are an experienced LoL player you will pick-up on this more quickly than others it seems, and do most of your combo's succesfully.

While I thought it would be really weird that my character would be controller this way, it felt natural quite quickly. League of Legends is a game that can be played on mobile and Wild Rift shows that. The better and more fluid version will always be the PC one, but I can't wait to play Wild Rift on the couch, train or during a lunch break at work. The game is well suited to play during the off time moments of the day. Especially since jumping into a game is really quick. While there is some ramp-up time on the PC version of the game, Wild Rift throws you into the game faster and also gives you tools to end the game quickly. The map is a bit smaller, while keeping the outlook of the original map and all jungle camps, and players get a constant stream of gold, just like in ARAM, to ramp up their power during the game. This means that the laning phase won't take long, and you'll all start fighting as soon as possible.

At this moment not all champions are part of the Wild Rift roster, but I do like the champions Riot chose to be in the game for now. It seems that they picked the most popular champions for each lane, while presenting a roster which varries in playstyle. In the mid lane for example, you can choose for a control mage like Ziggs or Oriana, while also Ahri and Yassuo are available if you want to play as an Assassin. The same variety can be found in the other lane and jungle, and keeps room for other champions to be added. The first to that will join the Wild Rift, are Draven and Darius.

Sad to say your progress from the PC version of the game won't carry over to Wild Rift, so your cool skins won't be available in both games. To compensate for this, Riot has a Rift to Rift event, which gives you a headstart on getting champions and skins for Wild Rift if you have played the PC version of the game before.

I really enjoyed my time with League of Legends Wild Rift and can't wait to jump into the action again when the game goes into open beta on december 10th. While having played mostly Ahri during the test weekend, I want to grind and get Akali, as well as some Jungle champions.

See you on the rift, Summoner!
