The Wolf Among Us Review

A stylish new fantasy adventure.

BY CASPER TINZS

Telltale Games has over the years honed its skills over in making adventure games with consequential narratives and distinctive visuals, culminating in the acclaimed first season of games based on the comic book series The Walking Dead. Now, the team is tackling another comic in its video game adaptation of Bill Willingham’s Fables, with a story centered on Bigby Wolf, the sheriff of a magical version of New York City populated by fairytale characters. This is no fanciful romp through children's stories; Fables is a dark fairytale, and The Wolf Among Us uses a system of choices and consequential events found in The Walking Dead game to make players face the grim reality of this world.

The Wolf Among Us Episode 1: Faith Review

Verdict: Telltale has once again delivered a well-written adventure, this time with an added dose of stylish noir presentation. Faith begins Sheriff Bigby's adventure with an introduction that’s funny, brutal, and mysterious in the best possible ways. If this inaugural episode of The Wolf Among Us is any indication of the rest of the season, then Telltale's latest adventure game is set to provide an adventure every bit as memorable as Lee and Clementine's.

The Wolf Among Us Episode 2: Smoke and Mirrors Review

Verdict: Smoke and Mirrors is a great second act to The Wolf Among Us. Its 90 minutes are packed with memorable characters and scenes, as well as some of the most agonizing decisions Telltale has ever forced me to make. And despite being a tad bit disappointed that the overall plot hasn’t been moved all that far forward, I’m still completely invested and more than a bit terrified to see how my tough decisions will affect the story going on.

The Wolf Among Us Episode 3: A Crooked Mile Review

Verdict: We're halfway through the first season of A Wolf Among Us, and A Crooked Mile represents a new high point in Telltale's take on Fables. It's a smart, brutal, and emotional episode that pushes the story forward and fleshes out nearly every player on the board. The way it made me feel wildly powerful, yet completely vulnerable resonated with me on a ton of different levels. I can't wait to see where Telltale allows me to take Bigby in Episode 4.

The Wolf Among Us Episode 4: In Sheep's Clothing Review

Verdict: The Wolf Among Us Episode 4: In Sheep’s Clothing still features Telltale’s strong writing, but is ultimately my least favorite chapter of the series so far. The lack of meaningful choices and scant story progression make Episode 4 feel unimportant in the grand scheme of things. But despite this bump in the road, I’m still incredibly anxious to see how Telltale wraps up its first season in Fabletown.

The Wolf Among Us Episode 5: Cry Wolf Review

Verdict: A wonderful cap to Telltale’s season. It made me not only want to replay the episode to see various outcomes, but the entire series to see if I had missed any clues planted early on. The writing is great, the action is satisfying, and living with the consequences of my decisions is fantastically powerful. Simply put, The Wolf Among Us’ finale provides some of the finest storytelling I’ve played in a long time.
